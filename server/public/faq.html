How does Jackschess help me as a player?

Jackschess gives you the ability to connect with any chess player in the world and all the necessary tools-- all in one screen--to create the perfect online chess environment. Not only can you review with coaches one-on-one or in groups, but you can also hop on to review a game you just played with your opponent, show your friends all over the world your last tourney games, and more.

There is a big disconnect between the learning experience you get in-person with other players and what you get online from sites like chess.com, ICC, chess24, etc. No matter how good technology gets, Tactics Trainer will never be as good as a titled player reviewing your games and openings with you. Jackschess brings that in-person experience to the internet.

How does Jackschess help me as a coach?

Jackschess will help you teach chess better online, and potentially get you more students.

Nobody likes having to manage Skype/ICC or chess.com in the same screen, not to mention clunky tools like TeamViewer or programs that only Windows users can download. Group lessons are always a mess, you don't have all the features you need, etc. Skype and those websites aren't meant for a chess-learning environment. Jackschess is. Jackschess isn't designed for multiple purposes; it is meant to create the optimal chess environment for players to connect and enjoy the game all over the internet.

Jackschess also features accounts specifically for coaches. Having an account lets you have your own Jackschess room at your own personal URL. You also get a coach's toolbox where you can save positions, puzzles, games, recrodings, etc. for later reuse in other lessons.

What's the difference between a Jackschess room and a jackschess account?

Great question.

A chess room is a place on the internet where you have video chat, a chess board, and a bunch of chess-specific features all in one screen. It is hosted at a unique URL that you can share to invite whomever you want. However, a chess room is hosted at a random URL, anyone can join it, and you cannot save any data like positions, puzzles, games, etc.

An account comes with your own profile to market yourself as a coach or student, and it comes with your own personal chess room. Your own chess room is hosted at a customized URL. For example, my username is "JimmyMow," so my room is http://jackschess.com/jimmymow/room. Because I have an account, that room is also protected by a password--or what I call a "room secret"--so that only the people to whom you give your secret can enter your room. Moreover, an account comes with a toolbox, with which you can save positions, games, puzzles, lessons, etc.
